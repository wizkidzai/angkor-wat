Discovering a Magical Place

About 900 years ago, King Suryavarman II built Angkor Wat in Cambodia to honor the god Vishnu. The temple was designed to look like a holy mountain and has walls covered with carvings of gods and stories. Angkor was part of a huge city with amazing canals and reservoirs that helped people grow rice all year. Over time, it changed from a Hindu temple to a Buddhist temple. By the 1400s, the city declined, and the jungle grew around it. But Angkor Wat was never forgotten.
